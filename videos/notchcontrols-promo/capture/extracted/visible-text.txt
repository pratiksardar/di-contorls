NotchControls — launch brief (no website; open-source GitHub project)

PRODUCT
NotchControls is a free, open-source macOS app that turns the MacBook notch into a
cockpit for work sessions. Features: system-wide mic mute (mutes every app at once,
one click at eye level, ⌥⇧M anywhere) · camera in-use indicator · teleprompter that is
INVISIBLE in screen shares and recordings · notifications from coding agents (Claude
Code, Codex — any CLI) the second one needs input or finishes, click a banner to land
in the right window · live dashboard of every agent session on the machine (working /
idle / needs-you, project names, one-click focus) · quiet hours + meeting-aware holds ·
light/dark themes · MIT licensed. github.com/pratiksardar/di-contorls

AUDIENCE
Developers running multiple AI coding agents, and content creators who record or
present on camera. Both live the same afternoon:

THE RELATABLE STORY (the promo's spine)
It's 2:47 PM. You're on a call. Your camera is on. Somewhere behind the meeting
window, three coding agents are working — and one of them has been sitting on a
permission prompt for twenty minutes. You don't know. You're also not sure you
unmuted. And the script for your demo is on a sticky note below the camera, so your
eyes keep dropping.

Everything you're anxious about is invisible. And the one part of your screen you
look at all day — the notch — does nothing.

NotchControls puts the answers there. Mic state at eye level, one click to mute
everything. A banner the moment an agent needs you — click it, you're in the right
window. Every session, live: who's working, who's blocked, who's done. A teleprompter
under the camera your audience can never see.

MESSAGE (the one thing)
Your notch tells you the second something needs you — and gives you back your mic,
your script, and your flow.

TONE
Quiet confidence, dry humor about a real anxiety. Not shouty SaaS. Dark, native-macOS
aesthetic: black island, orange = needs you, green = working, red = muted.
